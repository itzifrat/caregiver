FAQs for finding dog groomers near you

What is a dog groomer?

Dog groomers are specialized professionals in the animal care industry who play a critical role in keeping man's best friend looking and feeling their best. They provide services such as bathing, brushing, trimming nails, dematting fur, cleaning ears and teeth, cleaning paw pads and styling specific breeds of dogs based on breed standard. Additionally, dog groomers have an eye for pet health concerns like skin conditions, pain and inflammation which could signify larger issues a veterinarian may need to be consulted for. Many dog groomers look for signs of fleas or ticks that need attention as well. Therefore, it is important to find a skilled and trustworthy dog groomer for your pup as they will become an essential part of your dogs care routine.

How much does it cost to hire a dog groomer?

All dog groomers charge their own unique rates and where you live, the dog groomers level of experience, and the type of grooming services you require can all impact how much you spend. With that context top of mind, the average hourly rate for dog grooming services on Care.com is $14.69.

What should I look for when choosing a dog groomer?

When choosing a dog groomer, there are several important factors to consider. First, you'll want to make sure that the groomer is certified or licensed in their fieldthis will ensure they have undergone proper training and passed necessary tests demonstrating their knowledge of pet grooming techniques. Additionally, it's important that you feel comfortable with the particular dog groomerask questions about their policies and procedures so you can get an idea of how well they know their craft. You may also want to tour the facility prior to making your decision; make sure it's clean, modern, and well-equipped with all necessary tools for providing quality grooming services for your pet. Lastly, be sure that you feel comfortable leaving your pet in their careask about staff qualifications and experience as well as safety protocols in case of emergency or health concerns regarding your pet's wellbeing during grooming sessions.

What should I expect when my dog goes for a grooming appointment?

Typically during a dog grooming appointment, first your pup will be bathed using specialized cleansers formulated specifically for dogs' skin types; then they will likely receive any necessary haircuts (depending on breed), followed by brushing out mats or tangles in fur; often nails will be trimmed, teeth brushed if needed, ears cleaned, and fur blow dried before styling. Keep in mind that some breeds may require more than one visit depending on coat type/length and any special needs requested by the ownerso always ask about length of time required prior to booking an appointment.

How often should I bring my dog in for grooming?

How often your pooch will require dog grooming depends largely on your individual pets needs and lifestyle. Some breeds may require more frequent visits than others due to their specific coats or hair types. As a general rule of thumb, it is recommended that you bring your pup in for a professional grooming every three to four months; however this may need to be adjusted depending on how quickly their nails grow or if they have long fur that needs regular brushing and trimming.
