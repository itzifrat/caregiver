FAQs for finding child care centers near you

What is a child care center?

A child care center is a facility that provides professional supervision and care of children while their parents are away. This structured environment offers a safe, nurturing, and educational atmosphere that can improve the well-being of every child in attendance. Child care centers have experienced and trained employees who understand the growing developmental needs of young children. Social activities, early learning curriculums, physical activity and healthy meals are just some aspects of these community organizations that give kids the opportunity to learn, grow, and enjoy themselves with other peers outside of the home environment.

What are the benefits of child care centers?

Child care centers are becoming increasingly popular as a way of providing parents with secure and nurturing environments for their children. By attending a child care center, children have the opportunity to not only interact with other kids and build relationships but to gain valuable educational skills. Centers provide qualified and experienced staff that assist young minds with activities such as literacy, numeracy, problem-solving and creativity that can help them in both school and life. Socialization is another big benefit of child care centers; from group play to learning etiquette, kids learn patience and respect for others by gaining an understanding for different perspectives. Time spent in daycare offers kids the time needed for exploration and experimentation in order to discover the world around them. Child care center staff invest tireless energy into little ones so they go home with happy stories of structured fun.

What will my child do at a child care center?

Sending your child to a child care center is a fantastic opportunity for them to develop and grow. Not only will they interact with other children, they'll also be exposed to a range of activities that will stimulate their physical, mental and social development. From playing indoors and outdoors, participating in educational activities such as songs and craft projects, children at child care centers gain valuable early skills that set them up for future success.
